Progress Tracker

The Progress Tracker helps you view how students are moving through coursework in your class. It gives you a quick snapshot of what content a student has accessed, while also pointing you toward more detailed performance information when needed.

Overview

What the Progress Tracker Shows

The Progress Tracker allows you to see your students' progress in your class. The tile for each student indicates what content the student has accessed.

Please note that this is not a reflection of student performance. The percentage bar across the top reflects how much of the coursework the student has opened, not their course average and not the percentage of submitted work.

Also, the Assignments/Assessments data point on the tile reflects that the assignment has been opened, not that it has been submitted.

At a Glance

Student Tile View

Each student tile gives you a quick overview of course access and activity. Use it as a starting point when reviewing student engagement in the course.

What to Notice

How to Read the Tile

Progress Bar

Shows how much coursework the student has opened.

Assignments/Assessments

Shows that the assignment or assessment has been opened, not submitted.

Engagement Snapshot

Useful for seeing course access, but not enough on its own to measure performance.

Go Deeper

View More Meaningful Performance Data

To view more meaningful performance data, click the red icon in the top right corner of an individual student tile.

Step 1

Click the Red Icon

Use the red icon in the upper-right corner of the student tile to open the student-specific progress screen.

Step 2

Review Student Performance

On the individual student screen, you can see the student’s coursework average, grades on individual assignments, and the time spent on each lesson and assignment.

Individual Student View

Detailed Student Progress Screen

This screen gives you a more meaningful view of student performance than the overview tile alone.

Quick Takeaways

What to Remember

✓

Progress Tracker shows access, not mastery.

✓

The percentage bar is not a course grade.

✓

Opened does not mean submitted.

✓

Use the individual student view for more meaningful performance data.

Important Reminder

Use Progress Tracker as a Starting Point

The Progress Tracker is helpful for monitoring access and activity, but it should not be used by itself to judge student success. For performance details, always move into the individual student screen.
