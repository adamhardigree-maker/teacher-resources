Communication

Who Do Students Contact?

Students get help faster when they know who to contact. CVA teachers are the first stop for questions related to the class itself, while counselors and CVA support teams help with scheduling, credits, graduation planning, and additional layers of support.

Overview

Point Students to the Right Support

Teachers are the first stop for anything related to the class itself. If a student is confused about instructions, needs clarification on feedback, or wants help understanding the content, the teacher is the right person to contact.

When teachers consistently point students to the right place, two things happen: students get help faster, and teachers can focus on teaching rather than trying to solve every type of problem.

Quick Guide

Who Handles What?

Use these categories to help students contact the person who can best answer their question.

First Stop

Teacher

Teachers answer questions about the class, assignment directions, feedback, content, due dates, grading, and student progress in the course.

Additional CVA Support

Department Leads

Department Leads help support learning strategies and academic progress across courses. They serve as a bridge between the student and additional layers of CVA support.

Big-Picture Planning

School Counselor

School counselors address scheduling, credits, graduation requirements, transcripts, and college or career planning.

Teacher Support

Questions Teachers Should Answer

Teachers should be the first point of contact when the question is connected to classroom learning, assignments, feedback, or academic progress in the course.

Class Content

Answers questions about the class and helps students understand the content.

Assignment Directions

Clarifies assignment directions, expectations, rubrics, and requirements.

Feedback

Explains feedback on submitted assignments and helps students understand how to improve.

Skills and Concepts

Helps students understand a skill, concept, reading, problem, or task.

Due Dates and Grading

Answers questions about course due dates, grading expectations, and assignment status.

Academic Progress

Checks in on struggling students and helps them create a realistic plan to get back on track.

Learning Support

Study Tips and Online Learning Strategies

Teachers can also provide study tips and online learning support to help students manage their work and succeed in the course.

Time Management

Helps students plan their time and keep up with coursework.

Organization

Helps students organize tasks, assignments, and course expectations.

Getting Caught Up

Helps students create realistic plans when they fall behind.

Academic Success

Supports students with strategies that help them improve and move forward.

Teacher Language

Sample Responses

Use clear, supportive language to help students understand their next step.

Assignment Question

“That’s a great question about the assignment directions. Let me walk you through what the rubric says about the assignment.”

Falling Behind

“You've fallen behind, and reaching out is the first step! Let's work together to come up with a realistic schedule to get you back on track.”

Counselor Question

“That’s a great question about your graduation requirements. Your school counselor will be the best person to help with that.”

School Counselor

When Students Need Big-Picture Academic Planning

School counselors focus on big-picture academic planning rather than day-to-day coursework.

Course Scheduling

Questions about schedule changes, course placement, or future course plans.

Graduation Requirements

Questions about what a student needs to graduate.

Credits and Transcripts

Questions about credit checks, transcripts, and academic records.

College and Career Planning

Questions about post-secondary planning, pathway decisions, or future goals.

Important Reminder

Do Not Contact the Student’s Local School

CVA teachers should not contact a student’s local school directly. Direct all questions or concerns to Teacher Support or your Department Lead.
