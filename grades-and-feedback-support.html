CVA Teacher Resource

Helping Students Check Grades and Feedback

Use this guide when students ask about grades, missing work, resubmissions, or teacher feedback. The goal is to help students know where to look, what the icons mean, and what action to take next.

Teacher Focus

What Students Need to Understand

Students often check only the grade and miss the bigger picture. When supporting students, remind them that CTLS My Grades helps them track progress, view assignment status, and read feedback, while StudentVUE/Synergy shows the official grade of record. Students should use both, but for different purposes.

1

Daily Progress

CTLS My Grades

Students should use My Grades to check assignment status, view submitted work, look for missing assignments, see grades after items are reviewed, and read teacher feedback.

2

Official Grade

StudentVUE / Synergy

Students should use StudentVUE to view the official grade of record. Remind students that grades entered in CTLS may take up to 24 hours to appear in StudentVUE because of the overnight sync.

Suggested Teacher Language

What to Say to Students

“Before you email me about a grade, please check two places.”

First, go to CTLS My Grades. This is where you can see assignment status, submitted work, grades, missing assignments, and feedback from me.

Then, check StudentVUE for your official grade of record. If I graded something today, you may see it in CTLS right away, but it may not appear in StudentVUE until after the overnight sync.

If something still does not make sense, send me a message with the assignment name, what you checked, and your specific question.

Student Directions

Direct Students to Do This First

CTLS Gradebook Icons

Help Students Read the Icons

Students may see more than one icon in My Grades. That is normal. The icons are visual shortcuts that help students understand the status of their work and what to do next.

Missing

The assignment was not submitted before the due date. Direct the student to open the assignment, complete the work, and submit it in CTLS.

Finished

The assignment was submitted and graded. Direct the student to review the grade and any feedback.

Submitted

The work has been submitted and is waiting for teacher review. Remind the student that grading may not be immediate.

Resubmission

The assignment has been reopened. Direct the student to make changes and submit the work again.

Assigned

The assignment is available but has not been started. Direct the student to open the directions and begin.

In Progress

The student has started the assignment but has not submitted it yet. Direct the student to finish and submit.

Feedback

The assignment has feedback available. Direct the student to open and read the feedback before asking about the grade.

Feedback Locations

Where Students Should Look for Feedback

Feedback may appear in different places depending on the type of assignment. Use the sections below to coach students through the most common feedback locations.

Assessment Feedback

For quizzes, tests, or assessments, students may see Instructor Feedback and feedback for individual questions. Direct students to open the graded assessment from Coursework or My Grades and review both areas carefully.

Assignment and Attachment Feedback

For assignments, students should look for Instructor Feedback. Feedback may be written, audio, or video.

If the student uploaded a file, direct them to open the attachment under My Attachments. Teachers may annotate directly on the document, so students may need to view or download the file to see all comments.

Discussion Feedback

Students may see public comments on the discussion board. These comments can be read by others in the class.

Teachers may also leave private Instructor Feedback. Direct students to scroll to the bottom of the page to check for private feedback that only they can see.

Copy and Paste Support

Quick Teacher Response Bank

When a Student Says, “I Don’t Know My Grade”

Please check CTLS My Grades first. That will show your assignment status, grades, missing work, and any feedback I have left. Then check StudentVUE for your official grade of record.

When a Student Says, “I Submitted It, But It Still Looks Different in StudentVUE”

Thank you for checking. Grades entered in CTLS may take up to 24 hours to appear in StudentVUE because of the overnight sync. Check CTLS for the most recent assignment-level update, and check StudentVUE again after the sync.

When a Student Says, “Why Did I Get This Grade?”

Please open the assignment in CTLS and read the feedback I left for you. Be sure to check Instructor Feedback, any attachment comments, and any notes connected to the assignment. After you review the feedback, send me a specific question if something is unclear.

When a Student Has Missing Work

Go to CTLS My Grades and look for assignments marked Missing or Assigned. Open the assignment from Coursework, review the directions, complete the work, and submit it in CTLS.

When a Student Has a Resubmission

I reopened the assignment so you can make changes and submit again. Please read the feedback first, revise your work, and then resubmit the assignment in CTLS.

Student Communication Habit

Ask Students to Include These Details

When students message about a grade or feedback question, encourage them to include enough information for you to respond quickly and accurately.

Message Template for Students:

Hi [Teacher Name], I have a question about [assignment name]. I checked CTLS My Grades and saw [what I noticed]. I also checked StudentVUE and saw [what I noticed]. I read the feedback and my question is [specific question]. Thank you!

Important Reminder

Feedback Is Part of the Learning Process

When students check grades, guide them to check feedback too. The grade tells them how they performed on one assignment, but the feedback helps them understand what to improve before the next one.
