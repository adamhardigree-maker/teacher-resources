CVA Teacher Resource

Helping Students Check Grades and Feedback

Use this guide when students ask about grades, missing work, resubmissions, or teacher feedback. The goal is to help students know where to look, what the icons mean, and what action to take next.

Teacher Focus

Help Students Know Where to Look

Students often check only the grade and miss the bigger picture. When supporting students, remind them that CTLS My Grades helps them track assignment-level progress and feedback, while StudentVUE/Synergy shows the official grade of record. Students should use both, but for different purposes.

Daily Progress

CTLS My Grades

Students should use My Grades to check assignment status, submitted work, missing assignments, graded items, and teacher feedback.

Teacher reminder: Direct students here first when they ask about a specific assignment, missing work, resubmissions, or feedback.

Official Grade

StudentVUE / Synergy

Students should use StudentVUE to view the official grade of record for the course.

Teacher reminder: Grades entered in CTLS may take up to 24 hours to appear in StudentVUE because of the overnight sync.

Simple message for students: Use CTLS My Grades to understand what is happening with each assignment. Use StudentVUE to confirm your official course grade.

Quick Guide

What Do the Gradebook Icons Mean?

Open each card to see what the icon means and what you may need to do next.


Missing Click to learn more 

The assignment was not submitted before the due date. Direct the student to open the assignment, complete the work, and submit it in CTLS. This means you did not submit the assignment before the due date.


Finished Click to learn more 

The assignment was submitted and graded. Direct the student to review the grade and any feedback. This means you submitted the assignment, and your teacher has graded it.


Submitted Click to learn more 

The work has been submitted and is waiting for teacher review. Remind the student that grading may not be immediate. This means you submitted the assignment, and it is waiting for your teacher to review and grade it.


Resubmission Click to learn more 

The assignment has been reopened. Direct the student to make changes and submit the work again. This means the teacher has reopened the assignment, and you can submit it again.


Assigned Click to learn more 

The assignment is available but has not been started. Direct the student to open the directions and begin. This means the assignment has been given to you, but you have not started it yet.


In Progress Click to learn more 

The student has started the assignment but has not submitted it yet. Direct the student to finish and submit. This means you have started the assignment and are working on it, but have not submitted it yet.


Feedback Click to learn more 

The assignment has feedback available. Direct the student to open and read the feedback before asking about the grade. This means your assignment has been graded and your teacher has left feedback for you to review.

Feedback Locations

Where Students Should Look for Feedback

Feedback may appear in different places depending on the type of assignment. Use these sections to help students check the right area before they ask about a grade or resubmission.

Teacher reminder: Encourage students to read the feedback before messaging about the grade. The grade shows the result, but the feedback explains what to improve.

Assessment Feedback +

For quizzes, tests, or assessments, students may see Instructor Feedback and feedback for individual questions.

What to tell students: Open the graded assessment from Coursework or My Grades and review both the overall instructor feedback and any question-level feedback.

Assignment and Attachment Feedback +

For assignments, students should look for Instructor Feedback. Feedback may be written, audio, or video.

If the student uploaded a file, direct them to open the attachment under My Attachments. Teachers may annotate directly on the document, so students may need to view or download the file to see all comments.

What to tell students: Check Instructor Feedback first. Then open any returned attachment to look for comments, markings, or annotations directly on the file.

Discussion Feedback +

Students may see public comments on the discussion board. These comments can be read by others in the class.

Teachers may also leave private Instructor Feedback. Direct students to scroll to the bottom of the page to check for private feedback that only they can see.

What to tell students: Review any public discussion comments, then scroll to the bottom of the page to check for private Instructor Feedback.

Teacher Response Bank

What to Say When Students Ask

Use these quick responses when students ask about grades, missing work, feedback, or resubmissions. Each response points students back to the correct place to check and gives them a clear next step.

Q

Student Question

“I don’t know my grade.”

A

Teacher Response

Please check CTLS My Grades first. That will show your assignment status, grades, missing work, and any feedback I have left. Then check StudentVUE for your official grade of record.

Q

Student Question

“I submitted it, but StudentVUE still looks different.”

A

Teacher Response

Thank you for checking. Grades entered in CTLS may take up to 24 hours to appear in StudentVUE because of the overnight sync. Check CTLS for the most recent assignment-level update, and check StudentVUE again after the sync.

Q

Student Question

“Why did I get this grade?”

A

Teacher Response

Please open the assignment in CTLS and read the feedback I left for you. Be sure to check Instructor Feedback, any attachment comments, and any notes connected to the assignment. After you review the feedback, send me a specific question if something is unclear.

Q

Student Question

“What do I do about missing work?”

A

Teacher Response

Go to CTLS My Grades and look for assignments marked Missing or Assigned. Open the assignment from Coursework, review the directions, complete the work, and submit it in CTLS.

Q

Student Question

“My assignment was reopened. What do I do?”

A

Teacher Response

I reopened the assignment so you can make changes and submit again. Please read the feedback first, revise your work, and then resubmit the assignment in CTLS.
