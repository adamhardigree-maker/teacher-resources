Online Teaching Practices

Weekly Facilitation Routine

In an asynchronous course, students experience their teacher primarily through grading, feedback, and communication — not through a shared classroom space. A consistent weekly routine is what makes that presence visible, predictable, and effective.

Big Idea: Async teaching requires intentional structure. A predictable routine is what makes responsive, student-centered instruction sustainable.

Overview

Teaching Online Is Ongoing, Not Event-Based

In a traditional classroom, the bell schedule creates natural rhythms. In an asynchronous environment, teachers create their own. Strong CVA teachers don't wait for students to fall behind before taking action. They build routines that surface concerns early, keep communication flowing, and make feedback feel like a consistent part of the course experience.

Core Routine

A Framework for the CVA Teaching Week

The tasks below represent the core habits of effective CVA facilitation. Not every task happens every day, but all of them should happen every week.

1

Check Progress and Identify Concerns

Use the Progress Tracker in CTLS to review student activity across your classes. Look for students who have not accessed the course recently, are falling behind the Class Schedule, or have missing work accumulating.

In an online course, silence is not neutral. A student who stops showing up in the gradebook needs outreach — not a waiting period.

2

Grade and Return Feedback

Work toward returning graded feedback within CVA's 48-hour expectation. Prioritize recently submitted work and any assignments approaching feedback deadlines. When you grade, leave feedback — not just a score.

Grades without feedback are missed teaching moments. In an async environment, your written feedback is often the only instructional conversation a student receives.

3

Communicate Proactively

Reach out to students who are falling behind, missing work, or disengaged before the concern becomes a crisis. Post a Class Announcement when there is something timely students need to know — an upcoming deadline, a common mistake you are noticing, or a word of encouragement.

Students should hear from you before they feel lost. Proactive communication signals that you are present and that their success matters to you. Don't just rely on email; use CTLS Parent and voice calls to reach students and families.

4

Respond to Messages

Check and respond to student and family messages within 24 hours. Review any emails or messages forwarded through your Department Lead or sent by CVA Leadership. Do not let messages sit unanswered into a second day.

Response time is presence. In an online course, a prompt reply can be the difference between a student who pushes through and a student who disengages.

5

Review Your Gradebook

At least once a week, scan your gradebook for accuracy. Look for missing grades, assessments stuck In Progress, or patterns in student performance that might signal a need for intervention or a shift in your feedback approach. Check both CTLS and Synergy to ensure assignments and settings are correctly applied.

Your gradebook is the primary window students and families have into academic progress. Keeping it accurate and current is part of your instructional practice and responsibility as a teacher. Contact CVA Teacher Support right away if you notice any discrepancies between the CTLS Grading Center and Synergy Gradebook. Remember that Synergy is the gradebook of record for all CCSD students.

Why This Matters

Student Presence

Consistent routines help students feel seen and supported even in a fully async environment.

Early Intervention

Regular progress checks let teachers catch concerns before they become withdrawal risks.

IPR Alignment

Each routine area connects directly to the domains reviewed in the Instructional Practice Review.

Quick Takeaways

What to Remember

✓
Async teaching requires you to create your own rhythm — the schedule won't do it for you.
✓
Check the Progress Tracker regularly so you can reach out before students fall too far behind.
✓
Feedback, communication, and gradebook accuracy are weekly responsibilities, not monthly ones or tasks left to the end of the term.
✓
A consistent routine is the foundation of CVA Teaching Expectations and a strong student experience.
Important Reminder

Routine Is What Makes This Work

No single week will be perfect. What distinguishes effective CVA teachers is not perfection. It is consistency. Students in asynchronous courses succeed when they can count on their teacher to show up, respond, and follow through, week after week.
