Instructional Practice Review

The Instructional Practice Review, or IPR, is a structured process CVA uses to review instructional practices in an asynchronous learning environment. It reflects a straightforward core value: instructional growth is a collaborative process rather than a checklist.

Overview

A Growth-Oriented Review Process

The IPR focuses on observable evidence of teaching practice, including purposeful communication, intentional feedback, student engagement, and professional responsibilities.

It also reflects the realities of online instruction by reviewing course facilitation, responsiveness, and the overall student experience.

Purpose

Feedback from the IPR Is Used To...

1

Identify Strengths in Instructional Practice

Recognize areas where teaching practices support a strong online learning experience for students.

2

Clarify and Reinforce Instructional Expectations

Provide clear, consistent expectations for communication, feedback, student support, and professional responsibilities.

3

Guide Professional Growth and Instructional Coaching

Support reflection, coaching conversations, and targeted next steps for continued instructional growth.

4

Inform Professional Learning and Teacher Support

Help CVA identify common needs, plan professional learning opportunities, and strengthen teacher support.

Important Note

Supportive and Growth-Oriented

While the IPR is designed to be supportive and growth-oriented, it serves as an important measure of professional practice, informing decisions related to professional development, class assignments, and instructional support.

Review Domains

What the IPR Reviews

The IPR is organized around key areas of online teaching practice that shape the student experience in CVA courses.

Domain 1

Professionalism & Collaboration

Reviews professional conduct, collaboration, CVA protocols, communication with team members, and responsibilities that support student success.

Domain 2

Communication & Responsiveness

Looks at how clearly, consistently, and professionally teachers communicate with students and families in the online environment.

Domain 3

Rapport & Relationships

Focuses on how teachers build positive connections, encourage belonging, and help students feel known and supported.

Domain 4

Grading & Feedback

Reviews timely grading, meaningful feedback, gradebook updates, academic integrity expectations, and feedback that supports student growth.

Domain 5

Proactive Intervention & Student Support

Focuses on monitoring student progress, identifying concerns early, reaching out when students fall behind, and using timely support to promote success.

Observable Evidence

What Reviewers Look For

✓
Purposeful communication that helps students and families understand expectations.
✓
Intentional feedback that helps students understand their progress and next steps.
✓
Student engagement supported through outreach, encouragement, and meaningful learning interactions.
✓
Professional responsibilities such as following CVA procedures, protecting student privacy, and responding appropriately.
✓
Course facilitation and responsiveness that reflect the realities of asynchronous online instruction.

Rubric Structure

How Practice Is Described

The IPR rubric describes instructional practice across levels of growth. These levels help clarify expectations, support coaching conversations, and identify next steps.

Building Skills

Identifies foundational expectations and practices needed for consistent CVA instruction.

Supporting Learning

Describes practices that support student clarity, connection, growth, and progress.

Effective Teaching

Highlights consistent, intentional, and student-centered online teaching practices.
