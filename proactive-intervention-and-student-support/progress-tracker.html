Navigating CTLS

Progress Tracker

The Progress Tracker helps you view how students are moving through coursework in your class. It gives you a quick snapshot of what content a student has accessed, while also pointing you toward more detailed performance information when needed.

Overview

What the Progress Tracker Shows

The Progress Tracker allows you to see your students' progress in your class. The tile for each student indicates what content the student has accessed. Use it as a starting point when reviewing student engagement in the course.

What to Notice

How to Read the Tile

Progress Bar

Shows how much coursework the student has opened, not their Coursework Average and not the percentage of submitted work.

Coursework Average

Shows the student's current average score in the class, according to the CTLS Grading Center. This is not the gradebook of record; however, a student's average in the CTLS Grading Center should match the grade in StudentVUE/Synergy.

Assignments and Assessments

Shows that the assignment or assessment has been opened, not submitted.

Engagement Snapshot

The tiles will alert you to the Date Started and the Last Activity Date. Useful for seeing course access, but not enough on its own to measure performance.

Go Deeper

Detailed Student Performance Data

This screen gives you a more meaningful view of student performance than the overview tile alone. Each student tile gives you a quick overview of course access and activity. Use it as a starting point when reviewing student engagement in the course.

Step 1

Click the Red Icon

Use the red icon in the upper-right corner of the student tile to open the student-specific progress screen.

Step 2

Review Student Performance

The individual student tiles will alert you to the Date Started and the Last Activity Date. You will also be able to tell what Unit they are currently on, Page, and how many Assignments/Assessments, Pages, and Discussions they have completed. On the individual student screen, you can see the student’s Coursework Average, Grades on individual Assignments, and the Time Spent on each lesson and assignment.

Quick Takeaways

What to Remember

✓

Progress Tracker shows access, not mastery.

✓

The percentage bar is not a course grade.

✓

Opened does not mean submitted.

✓

Use the individual student view for more meaningful performance data.

Important Reminder

Use Progress Tracker as a Starting Point

The Progress Tracker is helpful for monitoring access and activity, but it should not be used by itself to judge student success.
